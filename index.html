Ali & Nino

Officially known as "Man and Woman", this moving statue slowly brings two lovers together for a short embrace, only for them to part almost immediately. It stands tall in at the coast of the Black Sea in Batumi, Georgia. The coming together and breaking apart of the 8-meter-high steel figures takes almost a full 10 minutes. The sculpture was created by famous Georgian artist and sculptor Tamara Kvesitadze.

To the people of Batumi, the statue is known as Ali and Nino, after the novel written by Azerbaijan writer Kurban Said. It's a familiar tale of lovers who end up in tragic circumstances that keep them apart. As opposed to warring families, like Romeo and Juliet, in Ali and Nino’s case, it was the first World War. Ali, an Azerbaijani Muslim, falls in love with Georgian princess, Nino, but sadly, after they are finally able to get together, the war hits home and Ali is killed.

The surrounding mysteries

Nobody is entirely sure how the story of Ali and Nino became associated with Tamara Kvesitadze’s statue.
